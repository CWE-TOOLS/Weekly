Batching Ticket Generator

Floor Cheat Sheet
You enter the numbers — it builds the batches & tickets.

1 What you enter

  • Total Cu Ft — material the casting needs (required)
  • Face Sq Ft — sets how much is the face coat
  • Cu Ft / 250 lb batch — defaults to 4.28
  • Pigment reduction % — defaults to 50%
  • Batched by & Notes — print on the ticket
Can auto-import Cu Ft & Face Sq Ft from inventory — only if every row has both filled in.

2 How many batches

  • Sand sizes: 250 / 150 / 100 lb
  • Uses the fewest, largest batches that cover the volume
  • A leftover bit adds one more small batch
  • 150 + 100 → combined into one 250 (one less batch to mix)
  • Listed largest first

3 How batches are labeled

Direct Cast 50/50 split: FACE + FINAL BACK UP. No First Back Up.
Spray Up
(Face Sq Ft entered)		 FACE fills the face volume (3/16" thick) → FIRST BACK UP ≈ same again → rest is FINAL BACK UP.
Spray Up
(no Face Sq Ft)		 First = FACE, last = FINAL BACK UP, middle = FIRST BACK UP.

4 Recipe scaling

  • The color log is the recipe — it must have a Sand entry
  • Every ingredient scales by batch sand ÷ color-log sand
  • e.g. 100 lb batch off a 250 lb recipe = 40% of everything

5 Automatic material rules  ★

The generator applies these for you — you don't adjust them by hand.
Bulk Sand swap
Spray Up back-up batches show sand as "Bulk Sand (Cowbay)." Face uses the color-log sand.
Fibers → Cemfill
Spray Up: back-ups use Cemfill. Direct Cast: all batches use Cemfill. Amount = 18 lb per 250 lb batch (scales down). Otherwise fiber is straight from the color log.
Pigment reduction
FINAL BACK UP always reduced (50% default). FIRST BACK UP only if you check the box. FACE always full pigment.

6 Mix Day totals

  • Adds up all batches so you know what to pull
  • Uses the same sand / Cemfill / pigment rules above
  • Grouped: Dry Batch · Pigments · Additives

7 The printed tickets

  • One page per batch, color-coded:
FACE = Orange  FIRST B/U = Blue  FINAL B/U = Yellow
  • Has Dry Batch, Pigments & Additives tables
  • Blanks for Air/Water Temp & Ice on the floor
  • Slump target bullseye — default Face 6", Back-up 5"

8 Won't print if…

  • No Total Cu Ft entered
  • No Sand entry in the color log
These guards stop a ticket from being built on missing info.
Bottom line: You give volume, face area & batcher — it decides batch count, labels each, scales the recipe, applies the rules, and prints a color-coded ticket per batch. Releasability / Casting Tools
