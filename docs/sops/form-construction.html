Standard Operating Procedure Form Construction

General guidelines for building the forms a casting is cast in — which sheet stock to use, when to spend on the durable materials, and how rails, brackets, and tread strips go together. These are defaults, not hard rules: the right choice mostly comes down to how many times the form will be cast.
Melamine is moisture-sensitive. Even a small scratch through the laminate face lets the flakeboard core soak up water and swell — a scratched or gouged melamine form should not be reused. Keep faces clean and dry, and move to HDO or Blackboard for anything that will be cast more than once.

1 Sheet goods stocked in-house

Forms are generally built from one of four sheet goods kept in stock:

StockSizeCoreFacing
Melamine 4' × 8' × ¾" Flakeboard White laminate
Melamine 5' × 9' × ¾" Flakeboard White laminate
HDO 4' × 8' × ¾" Birch Tan laminate
Blackboard / EuroForm 5' × 12' × ¾" Birch Black laminate

Blackboard / EuroForm is typically custom-ordered per project rather than pulled from general stock.

2 Choosing the stock — how many casts?

One-time casting → Melamine
Forms used for a single casting are generally built from melamine. HDO is cost-prohibitive on one-time work, and melamine gives a clean face for one pour. Because it's moisture-sensitive (see the warning above), treat a one-time melamine form as disposable.
Rebuilt 3+ times → HDO or Blackboard
Forms that will be rebuilt or reused three or more times are generally built from HDO or Blackboard. The birch core and tougher facing hold up to repeated castings where melamine would fail.

3 Rails & brackets

Rails
Forms are built up from side walls called rails. They set the perimeter and height of the cavity.
Rails over 2"
Any rail taller than 2" needs a bracket behind it to keep it from bowing under the pour — generally spaced every 8" along the rail.
Bracket material
Brackets are always made from HDO — preferably used stock pulled from the rack in the forms tent before cutting into new sheets.

4 Stair-tread strips

Strips on stair treads are cut from Azek sheet material and applied to the base of the tread with two-sided tape.

Bottom line: melamine for one-time forms, HDO or Blackboard for anything cast three or more times; brace rails over 2" with HDO brackets every 8", and cut tread strips from Azek. SOP 8.1 — Form Construction
