6.2

Standard Operating Procedure Mill and Form Out Staging

Rev: 1.0
Cadence: Weekly — before Team Leader buy-in
Owner: ____________
Fill out staging descriptions for upcoming Mill and Form Out tasks in the weekly schedule so Management can objectively track completion of the work for that day, and Team Leaders can accurately buy into the upcoming work for the following week.

Mill tasks typically entail

Cutting parts for forms (table saw and CNC), applying Formica, cutting foam, applying bondo or epoxy, checking over / cleaning forms to be rebuilt, etc.

Form Out tasks typically entail

Assembling forms, assembling subcomponents, reconfiguring or rebuilding previously used forms, etc.
1

Stage the descriptions on the Mill and Form Out rows of the Project Portal's Staging tab. For the grid mechanics — casting pills, adding days, copy/paste, autosave — see SOP: Inputting Staging

2

Reconfirm the form quantities. Piece counts auto-populate from the Project Portal (Casting Inventory quantity + extras) — confirm they are correct at the time the staging is created.

3

Write each Mill day's description: the key steps of the parts milling process, quantifying the forms the parts are needed for (ex: Cut all bases, Cut all rails).

4

Include any key details important to the build in the Mill staging. (ex: epoxy form, apply Formica to foam, check rebuild forms)

5

Write each Form Out day's description: assembling forms, assembling subcomponents, reconfiguring or rebuilding previously used forms.

6

Multi-day Mill / Form Out processes: the staging must specifically describe what work will be completed on Day 1 and what on Day 2. Decide which way the work splits and say so:

• A certain number of forms fully completed each day (ex: Day 1 — build 12 of 25 forms; Day 2 — build remaining 13), or
• All forms brought up to a certain step on Day 1 (ex: Day 1 — cut all parts for all forms; Day 2 — assemble all forms).

The department is shown by the card color (Mill = cyan, Form Out = blue). Each line you write in Staging prints as its own line on the card; the piece count band fills in from Casting Inventory.

Bottom line: every upcoming Mill and Form Out task carries a staging description that names the key steps, quantities, and build details for that day. SOP 6.2 — Mill and Form Out Staging
