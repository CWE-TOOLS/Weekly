Staging

Write the task descriptions for each casting ahead of time — broken down by department and by day — before those tasks ever land on the weekly board. The descriptions you write here automatically show up on the matching scheduled tasks, so you author the “what to do” text once, early.

Set up the project first

Save the project, then add at least one casting in Casting Inventory. Staging needs castings before there's anything to stage.

What this tab is for

Staging lets you pre-write the instructions for a casting—department by department, day by day—so they're ready before the work is scheduled. Instead of typing descriptions onto tasks one at a time as they appear on the weekly board, you lay them all out here in advance.

These descriptions are shared with the Weekly Schedule board

What you write here isn't just a copy. The Weekly Schedule board matches each staged description to a scheduled task by three things: the casting #, the department, and the order of the days. When all three line up, the description you wrote appears automatically on that scheduled task. So stage it once here, and it shows up on the board for you.

How the workspace is laid out

Like Optimizer Hours, the project's castings appear as pills across the top, with per-casting columns below. Click a pill to bring that casting into view.

Inside a casting you get a department × day grid. The standard departments are pre-filled down the side:

Mill
Milling work.
Form Out
Building and setting the forms.
Cast
Pouring the casting.
Demold
Stripping the casting from the form.
Finish
Finishing work.
Seal
Sealing the casting.

Each department row has day columns — Day 1, Day 2, and so on. Click a day to expand it, then type the description for that department on that day.

Phased projects

If the project uses Phases, only the castings in the phase you're viewing appear as pills here. Switch phases to stage the rest.

The buttons

+ Add Day
Adds another day column to a department, for work that spans more than the days shown.
Remove Day
Removes a day column from a department.
Remove Department
Removes a whole department row from the casting if it doesn't apply.
Copy Staging
Copies one casting's entire scaffold — its departments, days, and the descriptions you've written.
Paste Staging
Pastes that copied scaffold and its descriptions onto another casting.

Staging the descriptions

  1. Pick the casting. Click its pill to bring it into view.
  2. Adjust the days. For each department, use + Add Day or Remove Day so the day columns match how long that department's work runs.
  3. Drop any departments you don't need. Use Remove Department on rows that don't apply to this casting.
  4. Write the descriptions. Click a day to expand it and type what to do for that department on that day.
  5. Repeat down the grid. Work through each department and each day until the casting is fully described.
Stage one, paste to the rest

When a project has a run of repetitive castings, fully stage just one of them — departments, days, and descriptions — then use Copy Staging and Paste Staging to drop the same scaffold onto the others. Adjust only where a casting differs.

Saving

The portal saves your typing automatically as you go — watch the save indicator near the top-right of the project header. There's no separate “Save” button to remember.
