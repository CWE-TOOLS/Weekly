Actual Labor

A side-by-side scorecard for the job. It puts the hours actually worked next to the Optimizer Hours estimate so you can see, at a glance, where the job ran over the plan and where it came in under.

Read-only — nothing to type here

There is nothing to fill in on this tab. The actual hours come from the shop's labor-tracking sheet, which is kept up to date outside the portal. This tab simply reads those hours and lines them up against the estimate. If a number looks wrong, it's corrected on the labor sheet, not here.

What this tab is for

The Optimizer Hours tab is your plan — what you expected each task to take. This tab is the report card. Once people have actually built the castings and logged their time, you can compare the two and ask: did the work take as long as we thought? The gap between the two numbers is the variance, and it's the whole point of this tab.

How to read the comparison

The tab shows a comparison grid, one row per casting and task. Each row lines up three numbers — the estimate, the actual, and the difference between them:

  1. Find the casting and task. Each row names a casting and the department or production phase the hours belong to.
  2. Compare the two hour columns. Optimizer hrs is what was budgeted; Actual hrs is what was logged.
  3. Read the variance. The Variance column is Actual minus Optimizer. A positive number means the task ran over the estimate; a negative number means it came in under.

The columns, in plain English

ColumnWhat it shows
CastingThe casting the hours were worked on.
Department / PhaseThe production department or phase the work belongs to (the task being measured).
Optimizer hrsThe estimated, budgeted hours for that task — pulled straight from Optimizer Hours.
Actual hrsThe hours actually logged on that task, from the shop's labor-tracking sheet.
VarianceActual minus Optimizer. Positive = over the estimate, negative = under.

When you'd use it

This tab is usually reviewed near the end of a job, around close-out, once the work is done and the time is fully logged. It's part of looking back at how the estimate held up — see Statuses & Workflow for where close-out fits in the project's life cycle.

Use the variance to sharpen future estimates

If the same task keeps coming in well over (or well under) the estimate across castings, that's a signal — not a one-off. Carry that lesson into the next job's Optimizer Hours so the plan starts closer to reality.
