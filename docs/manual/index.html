Project Portal — User Manual

Everything you need to run a job at Concreteworks East, from kickoff to ship date. This manual walks through the portal one tab at a time — built for new hires learning the ropes and for anyone who needs a quick refresher.

What the Project Portal is

The Project Portal is the central home for every concrete job. Each project is one casting job, identified by a unique Project #. Inside a project you'll find everything about that job in one place: the customer and scope, the individual castings and their panels, the mix recipe, batching tickets, production tracking, shop-floor layout, crating and shipping, labor estimates, and meeting notes.

The portal is the planning and paperwork side of the shop. It connects to the Weekly Schedule board (where the actual work gets scheduled day-to-day) and to the Releasability Board (where a project's readiness is reviewed).

Getting there

  1. Open the Weekly Schedule and click the Project Portal button in the top navigation.
  2. You'll land on the project list — every job, searchable and sortable.
  3. Click any project to open it, or click + New Project to start a new one.
  4. Inside a project, use the tabs down the left side to move between sections. Use ← Back to list (top-left) to return to the list.
Everything saves itself

There is no “Save” button to remember. The portal saves your changes automatically a moment after you type. Watch the small Saving… / Saved indicator at the top-right of the project header to confirm.

The shape of a project

Every open project has the same 12 tabs down the left, in this order. This manual has one page per tab:

TAB 1
Project Info
The cover sheet — number, name, customer, dates, scope, and status.
TAB 2
Job Memos
A dated log of decisions and events for the job.
TAB 3
Casting Inventory
Every casting and its panels — including remakes from rejects.
TAB 4
Tracking
Move each panel through the production phases; assign crates.
TAB 5
Casting Layout
Auto-arrange panels onto the casting tables; fine-tune by hand.
TAB 6
Shipping
Group panels into crates and print packing lists.
TAB 7
Optimizer Hours
Estimate labor hours per task, per casting.
TAB 8
Staging
Pre-write task descriptions that feed the weekly board.
TAB 9
Color Log
The mix recipe — cement, sand, aggregates, pigments, additives.
TAB 10
Batch Tickets
Generate scaled batching tickets from the color log.
TAB 11
Classroom (CR) Notes
The three classroom meetings, each with a standard agenda.
TAB 12
Actual Labor
Compare hours actually worked against the estimate.

A typical job, start to finish

You won't use the tabs strictly in order, but here's the usual flow so the pieces make sense:

  1. Kick off. Create the project and fill in Project Info. Run Classroom 1.
  2. Define the work. Add castings and panels in Casting Inventory. Build the Color Log recipe.
  3. Plan production. Estimate labor in Optimizer Hours, lay out the tables in Casting Layout, and generate Batch Tickets.
  4. Build & track. Pre-write descriptions in Staging, then check off phases in Tracking as panels move through the shop.
  5. Crate & ship. Assign panels to crates and print packing lists in Shipping.
  6. Close out. Review Actual Labor against the estimate and move the project's status to Closed Out.

Two ideas worth knowing up front

Status

Every project has a status that tracks where it is in its life: Kicked Off → Releasability → Approved → In Production → Shipped → Closed Out. It's shown in the list, on the cover sheet, and on the Releasability Board. Full details on Statuses & Workflow.

Phases

Big jobs can be split into phases (wings, delivery waves). When turned on, several tabs gain per-phase views. It's a one-way switch, so only enable it when you mean it — see Phases.
